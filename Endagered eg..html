The Endangered Animals


The Royal Bengal Tiger
The Bengal tiger is a population of the Panthera tigris
subspecies and the nominate tiger subspecies.It ranks among the biggest wild cats alive today.
Once ranging widely across Asia, from the eastern coast of Russia to
the Caspian Sea in the west, the majestic
tiger is now an endangered species due to poaching and habitat fragmentation
as a result of human activities and climate change.



The Blue Whale
The blue whale is a marine mammal and a baleen whale.
Reaching a maximum confirmed length of 29.9 meters and weighing up to 199 tonnes,
it is the largest animal known ever to have existed.Like other large whales,blue whales are
threatened by environmental change including habitat loss and toxics.
Blue whales are especially harmed by ship strikes and by becoming entangled in fishing gear.
